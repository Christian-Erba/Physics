Quantum Computing

Quantum computing is a computing paradigm based on the principles of quantum mechanics, which differs from classical computing (based on Boolean logic) in the way it processes and manipulates information. In a quantum computer, information is represented by qubits, units of information that can be in either the 0 state or the 1 state, but also a superposition of both values ​​simultaneously. This allows a quantum computer to process much more information in parallel than a classical computer. Another fundamental concept of quantum computing is entanglement, a quantum property that allows two or more qubits to be correlated in such a way that the state of one of them depends on the state of the others. This property allows quantum computers to solve some problems more efficiently than classical computers.
