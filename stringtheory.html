String Theory

String theory is a physical theory that replaces point particles with one-dimensional objects called strings. This theory tries to unify quantum mechanics and general relativity. There are several versions of string theory, including boson theory and superstring theory. String theory implies the existence of new dimensions beyond the three spatial and temporal ones we know. These dimensions could host parallel universes other than ours. This idea is called a multiverse and is also supported by quantum mechanics. The multiverse is a very speculative concept and has no experimental proof yet.

Poor cat!

Schrödinger's cat is a thought experiment devised by physicist Erwin Schrödinger in 1935 to illustrate a paradox of quantum mechanics. In this experiment, a cat is placed in a box sealed with a mechanism that can kill it with a certain probability. According to quantum mechanics, the cat would be in a superposition of dead and alive states until you open the box and observe the result.
